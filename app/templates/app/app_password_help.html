Using Gmail with Your App

Download PDF

Why Do You Need an App Password?

If you have 2-Step Verification enabled on your Google account, Google blocks sign-ins from apps that don’t support modern security standards — like 12bytes sending email. To fix this, Google allows you to generate a unique App Password for secure access.

Step-by-Step: Creating a Gmail App Password

  1. Go to your Google Account at myaccount.google.com.
  2. Click Security in the left-hand menu.
  3. Under the “Signing in to Google” section, make sure 2-Step Verification is turned on.
  4. After enabling 2-Step Verification, return to the same section and click App Passwords.
  5. You may be prompted to sign in again.
  6. Under “Select app”, choose Mail. Under “Select device”, choose Other and type something like 12bytes.
  7. Click Generate. Google will display a 16-character password.
  8. Copy this password and paste it into your app’s environment variable or settings as your email password.
Important: You only see the app password once. Make sure to store it securely.
